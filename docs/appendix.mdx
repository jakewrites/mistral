# Appendix, Source Artefacts

Before this assessment I produced an independent information-architecture redesign of the Mistral documentation. Those artefacts are the **evidence base for [Exercise 1](/exercise-1/executive-summary)** and the **reference material for [Exercise 4](/exercise-4-onprem-ia)**. They are preserved here in their original form so they can be inspected directly, not just summarised.

:::note Why this is in the submission
The assessment asks for visible judgement. These artefacts *are* the judgement, a full 420-page placement decision, a navigable IA, and a landing-page design, made before I knew how they would be presented. Embedding the originals lets a reviewer check the reasoning at source.
:::

## 1. Information Architecture tree

The complete proposed IA, navigable to leaf level: six intent-based top-level sections, per-section page counts, and a one-line "writer rule" for each. This is the target structure that the [Exercise 1 recommendations](/exercise-1/recommendations) move pages into.

## 2. Landing-page design

The proposed documentation home: a six-item primary nav, four persona entry points (with the **sovereign / on-prem deployment path featured**), a journey strip, and a featured-models grid. The persona entry points are the mechanism behind [R6](/exercise-1/recommendations#r6), personas as entry points, not top-level sections.

## 3. Recommendations document

The written strategy behind the redesign, executive summary, current-state analysis, seven named design principles, the recommended IA with page counts, a use-case taxonomy for Build, and the landing-page rationale.

<DownloadLink href="/artifacts/ia-recommendations.docx">Download the IA recommendations document (DOCX)</DownloadLink>

Its central theses, quoted directly:

> "The primary navigation should reflect how users work, orientation, building, evaluating, shipping, not how Mistral's teams are organised internally."

> "The current Developers section is a junk drawer."

Both are load-bearing in [Exercise 1](/exercise-1/executive-summary): the first is the basis of [R4](/exercise-1/recommendations#r4), the second of [R3](/exercise-1/recommendations#r3).

:::caution Two versions exist, by design
An earlier draft of the audit kept `Deploy`, `Le Chat`, and `Evaluate & Ship` as separate top-level sections and let `Reference` bloat to 176 pages. The delta is documented in the [Exercise 1 Appendix](/exercise-1/appendix#a-note-on-versioning) as evidence for [R5](/exercise-1/recommendations#r5).
