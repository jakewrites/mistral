# Appendix, Source Artefacts

The information-architecture redesign behind this submission is captured in three artefacts: the **evidence base for [Exercise 1](/exercise-1/executive-summary)** and the **reference material for [Exercise 4](/exercise-4-onprem-ia)**. They are embedded here so they can be inspected directly, not just summarised, and they track the audit and the live site's current product terminology (Vibe, Products). These artefacts show the six intent-based sections; they do not visualise the orthogonal **managed/self-hosted surface axis** (paired entry points, per-page surface tags, and version-gating; see [R1](/exercise-1/recommendations#r1)), which is specified as a design rather than shipped as a running prototype here.

:::note Why this is in the submission
The assessment asks for visible judgement. These artefacts *are* the judgement: a structural placement decision, a navigable IA, and a landing-page design. Embedding them lets a reviewer check the reasoning at source.
:::

## 1. Information Architecture tree

The complete proposed IA, navigable to leaf level: six intent-based top-level sections, per-section page counts, and a one-line "writer rule" for each. This is the target structure that the [Exercise 1 recommendations](/exercise-1/recommendations) move pages into (the managed/self-hosted surface axis is a later addition and is not drawn here).

## 2. Landing-page design

The proposed documentation home: a six-item primary nav, four persona entry points (with the **sovereign / on-prem deployment path featured**, which sets the self-hosted surface), a journey strip, and a featured-models grid. The persona entry points are the mechanism behind [R1](/exercise-1/recommendations#r1), personas as entry points rather than top-level sections, and they share the [Example Rewrite](/exercise-1/example-rewrite)'s job-first phrasing: the reader's question as the card title, the product name inside as the answer.

## 3. Recommendations document

The written strategy behind the redesign: executive summary, current-state analysis, seven named design principles, the recommended IA with page counts, a use-case taxonomy for Build, and the landing-page rationale. Its central theses:

> The primary navigation should reflect how users work (orient, build, evaluate, ship), not how Mistral names its products and audiences.

> The current Developers section is a catch-all.

Both are load-bearing in [Exercise 1](/exercise-1/executive-summary): together they are the basis of [R1](/exercise-1/recommendations#r1), the reorganisation.
