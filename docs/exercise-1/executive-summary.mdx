# Executive Summary

<p className="doc-meta">Exercise 1 · Documentation audit and strategy proposal · Scope: the 420 live pages of docs.mistral.ai</p>

The Mistral documentation is technically rich and, in places, excellent. Its problem is not content quality, it is **organisation**. The site is structured around how Mistral's products and teams are named, not around what a developer is trying to do. The result is duplication, misfiled guides, and a top navigation that asks the reader to understand Mistral's internal taxonomy before they can find a task.

This proposal presents an audit of all 420 live documentation pages and a prioritised plan to reorganise them around **user intent**, orientation, building, evaluating, and shipping, while leaving the underlying content largely intact.

## The core finding

> The current top navigation reflects how Mistral is organised internally, by product surface (Le Chat, Studio, Developers, Admin), rather than how a developer works: orient, build, evaluate, ship. Wherever the two disagree, the reader pays the cost.

Three structural problems follow from this:

1. **The same task exists at several URLs.** Quickstarts are duplicated across `getting-started/`, `developers/`, and `le-chat/` path prefixes, 9 exact duplicates and 11 redirect-worthy variants. This dilutes search, splits inbound links, and makes "which one is canonical?" a question the reader has to answer.
2. **Guides are filed under the thing they mention, not the job they serve.** Prompt engineering, sampling, and deployment guides live under **Models**, because they mention models, but a reader looking for them is *building*, not comparing model cards. 26 pages are misfiled this way.
3. **`Developers` is a junk drawer.** It is a top-level section defined by audience rather than task, so it accumulates anything vaguely developer-shaped and duplicates content that already has a home.

## The proposal in one sentence

Replace the audience- and product-named top navigation with a six-section, intent-based architecture, **Get Started · Models · Build · Platform · Admin · Reference**, with Cookbooks and the API Reference as utility links, and move each of the 420 pages to the section that matches the reader's job rather than the product's name.

## Why this is low-risk

This is an **information-architecture** change, not a content one. No page needs to be rewritten to execute it; pages move, duplicates collapse into canonical URLs with redirects, and a handful of guides change parent. That is what makes it worth doing now: high navigational payoff, bounded engineering effort, and no new writing debt.
