# Example Rewrite

This is the rewrite the brief asks for: the live [platform overview](https://docs.mistral.ai/getting-started/platform-overview), rewritten in full. It is the page where [the developer walk](/exercise-1/executive-summary#where-the-developer-path-broke) broke, and it carries the core problem in miniature: it orients by product where the reader arrives with a job. This is the *page-level* rewrite; the docs home page, which greets you with the same product cards, gets its redesign in the [Appendix landing-page design](/appendix#2-landing-page-design). The facts are the live page's, captured 6 July 2026. The frame is new, plus one addition the audit argues for.

**What's weak.** The writing is competent line by line. The frame is the problem.

- The opening promises intent and delivers inventory. "Pick the one that matches what you want to do" is followed by what Mistral makes, so the reader must already know what "Vibe", "Studio", and "Admin" mean to choose. The page knows this: its own Common questions open with "What's the difference between Vibe and Studio?", the exact confusion the page just created.
- The developer's job never appears. "Build an AI application" is on no card. A developer has to decode that a "developer console" named Studio is where building starts, which is precisely the API-key gate that stalled [the developer walk](/exercise-1/executive-summary#where-the-developer-path-broke).
- The least oriented reader is served last. Someone arriving from Le Chat, with no idea what happened to their product, finds "What changes for me?" collapsed at the bottom of the page, below three cards that never mention it.
- The orientation page speaks insider vocabulary: "durable workflows", "control plane", "OCR models", to the one audience guaranteed not to know the platform yet.
- Self-hosting does not exist here. Mistral ships open-weight models you can run on your own hardware and a self-hosted Vibe / Le Chat Enterprise, and sovereignty is its differentiator, yet the page that orients every new reader never says you can run Mistral yourself. This is the discoverability gap the [Considerations section](/exercise-1/recommendations#considerations) documents, at its most visible.

**What changed:** the same destinations and the same facts behind a different first question, plus one card the live page is missing.

- The cards are named by the reader's job (do work for me, build an application, run it for a team, run it myself), and each product name arrives as the answer to a job rather than a name to decode. That dissolves the top common question outright: "What's the difference between Vibe and Studio?" becomes two different cards a reader self-selects between.
- The developer's path is explicit and starts with the two actions [the developer walk](/exercise-1/executive-summary#where-the-developer-path-broke) showed matter: create a key, send a request. Studio is introduced as the console those happen in, which is [R1](/exercise-1/recommendations#r1)'s Studio rule applied in one sentence.
- The Le Chat reader is caught in the first card instead of a collapsed accordion at the bottom.
- Three of the five common questions dissolve into the framing. The two that remain ("Who should have the Billing role?", "Are Chat agents and Vibe Work Skills the same thing?") are Admin and Vibe questions, and move to the pages that own those concepts.
- The fourth card is the one addition, and it is argued for elsewhere in this proposal rather than invented here: the [Considerations section](/exercise-1/recommendations#considerations) requires the *whether* of self-hosting to be public even when the *how* is gated (what it is, when to choose it, the contact path), and the [appendix landing-page design](/appendix#2-landing-page-design) features the sovereign / on-prem entry point among its four for the same reason. Self-hosting cuts across products, which is exactly why it needs a job card rather than a product card.
- Insider vocabulary ("durable workflows", "control plane") defers to the product pages, where it lands on a reader who has already chosen. (The link labels are illustrative: in production they point at the live quickstarts and consoles.)

## Why this is the pattern, not the exception

Every page the restructure re-homes, the Developers content above all, follows the same shape:

1. **Re-parent** the page under the task it serves (the structural change).
2. **301-redirect** the old URL (no broken links).
3. Optionally, **tighten the opening** to name the audience, the outcome, and the neighbours (the framing change).

Steps 1 and 2 are mechanical and scriptable. Step 3 is the writing, and it is where a technical writer adds the value the restructure creates room for. A rewrite as deep as the one above is reserved for the few pages every reader passes through, the overview first among them. For the long tail, the opening carries most of the value, which is why step 3 stops there.
