# Open Questions

The recommendations are settled. These are the calls I'm *not* sure about, kept in the open on purpose. The fastest route to a better answer is to name the fork and say what would settle it.

1. **Products vs Platform, and whether Admin folds in.** Renaming Products to Platform and nesting Admin inside would tighten the nav to five, but it merges two jobs with two audiences (a Vibe user *using* the product, an IT admin *governing the org*), and org governance is cross-cutting, so it sits above the products rather than inside them. "Platform" is also a loaded word at Mistral: La Plateforme was the API platform's name until it became Studio, and reviving it would trade on a retired brand. *Leaning:* keep Admin separate. *Resolved by:* a card-sort, and confirming what "Platform" still means internally.
2. **Where the non-developer lives: Products or the help centre.** Mistral already runs a separate help centre (`help.mistral.ai`) alongside these developer docs, so the Vibe Chat/Work user could be served there rather than in Products. Either way, the content they share with developers (model choice, prompting, "is my data safe?") must not be authored twice and left to drift. The rule that holds regardless: whichever surface owns a shared page, the other links to it rather than copying it, the same single-sourcing discipline as the [self-hosted consideration](/exercise-1/recommendations#considerations), one surface over. *Leaning:* as checked on 6 July 2026, the help centre's live remit is account, subscription, troubleshooting, and trust (four collections: Help and Troubleshooting; Vibe; Trust, Security, Compliance; About our Models), and its Vibe collection itself points readers elsewhere for product documentation. So non-dev product *usage* belongs in Products (Vibe), which the live site already keeps UI-first, with the help centre owning account, billing, and support. *Still open:* the content-ownership map for the shared trust pages, where the two surfaces overlap today.
3. **The data-governance border.** Configurable features (the ZDR toggle, region selection, audit-log access) are things you *do*, so they belong in docs. Legal commitments (the GDPR-rights process, retention policy, certifications) are things Mistral *promises*, so they belong on a Trust or legal surface. The rule: docs document what you can do; Trust documents what Mistral commits. Border cases ("where is my data stored?") are dual and cross-linked. *Resolved by:* docs and legal reviewing the border cases together to agree an ownership map.
4. **A top-level home for the Vibe user.** The proposal serves the non-builder through Products (Vibe), next to the developer-facing Studio. Those are opposite readers, and one section holding both is a real navigation cost. An alternative gives the person who just wants to use AI their own top-level section (working label "Vibe with AI", not yet settled), so they never land among developer tooling. Two things keep this open. First, it is an audience-named section, the pattern the proposal otherwise avoids, though Products already bends that rule. Second, if Vibe leaves Products, where does Studio go? It could stay in a slimmed Products, or move into Get Started and Build as the console where API keys and the Playground live. This is the finer version of question 2: once the non-developer lives in the docs, do they get their own section, or a shelf inside Products? *Leaning:* unsure; the label and Studio's home both need work. *Resolved by:* a card-sort with non-technical users, after settling Studio's placement.
